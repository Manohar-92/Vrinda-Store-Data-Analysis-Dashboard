# 🛍️ Vrinda Store – Annual Sales Data Analysis

A complete data analysis and interactive dashboard project built entirely in **Microsoft Excel**, using real retail sales data from Vrinda Store.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

The goal of this project was to analyze Vrinda Store's annual sales data, clean and prepare the raw dataset, and build a dynamic dashboard that helps the business understand:

- Who their customers are
- Which states and channels drive the most revenue
- How sales trend across the year
- What the order fulfillment health looks like

## 🧹 Data Cleaning Steps

The raw data required several cleaning steps before analysis:

- Removed duplicate and blank rows
- Standardized gender column values (e.g., `M` → `Men`, `W` → `Women`)
- Normalized age groups into categories: `Adult`, `Senior`, `Teenager`
- Corrected inconsistent state names and channel labels
- Formatted the `Amount` column as currency
- Ensured `Order Date` column was in proper date format for monthly grouping

## 📊 Dashboard Features

The final dashboard includes **6 interactive charts** with slicers for **Month**, **Category**, **Channel**, and **Gender**:

| Chart | Type | Insight |

## 📈 Key Insights

- **Women** contribute **64%** of total sales vs 36% from men
- **March** was the highest sales month of the year
- **Maharashtra** topped state-wise revenue at ₹29.9L, followed by Karnataka (₹26.5L)
- **Amazon** leads all channels with **35%** of total orders; Flipkart follows at 22%
- **Adult women** are the biggest buyer segment at **34.59%**
- **92%** of all orders were successfully delivered
- Only **8%** of orders had issues — cancellations (3%), returns (3%), refunds (2%)

## 💡 Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis:

1. **Target women aged 18–45** — they are the dominant buyer segment
2. **Focus marketing efforts in Maharashtra, Karnataka, and Uttar Pradesh** — top 3 revenue states
3. **Invest more in Amazon and Flipkart listings** — they drive 57% of combined orders
4. **Run promotions in Jan–Feb** to counter the post-March sales dip
5. **Investigate teenager segment** — currently the smallest group, potential growth opportunity
